The "Ghost Writer" Engine

1. Event-Driven Architecture

EcoSynch sits dormant until a specific event occurs in your Jira workflow. We do not use polling (which slows down Jira) or scheduled scraping (which is outdated).

We utilize an Event-Driven Serverless Architecture on AWS. When a ticket is completed, a secure, encrypted signal is sent to our backend within milliseconds.

2. The Lazy Indexingâ„¢ System

Most bots index your entire wiki upfront. This is invasive and costly. EcoSynch is smarter.

We use a proprietary "Lazy Indexing" algorithm. We only read and vectorize a Confluence page the first time it is involved in a sync. If the page content hasn't changed since the last sync, we skip the heavy processing entirely, saving you money and reducing API load.

3. Semantic Analysis (RAG)

We don't just keyword match. We use Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to understand context.

If your ticket says "Fixed the login bug," and your documentation says "Users may experience login errors," simple keyword matching might miss the connection. Our Vector Database understands that these concepts are semantically opposite, identifying exactly where the update is needed.

4. Surgical Rewriting

We send the outdated paragraph and your new Jira ticket to Google Gemini 2.0 Flash.

The AI acts as a "Technical Editor." It rewrites only the outdated sentence to match the new reality, preserving your original tone and formatting. Finally, it leaves a changelog comment for full transparency.
